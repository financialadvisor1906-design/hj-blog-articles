# End-of-Year Tax Planning: Strategies for Small Business Owners

![End-of-Year Tax Planning: Strategies for Small Business Owners](images/day_07.jpg)

*Day 7 of 30 | Category: Tax Strategy | 8 min read*

---

The final months of the calendar year represent the most critical window for small business tax planning. Unlike individual taxpayers who have limited control over their tax situation, business owners have significant flexibility to accelerate deductions, defer income, and implement strategies that can dramatically reduce their tax bill — but only if they act before December 31.

## Why Year-End Planning Matters

Tax planning is fundamentally different from tax preparation. Tax preparation is reactive — you report what happened. Tax planning is proactive — you make decisions that shape your tax outcome. The most effective planning happens in October, November, and December, when you have enough of the year behind you to project your income and enough time left to take action.

## Strategy #1: Accelerate Deductions

If you expect to be in the same or higher tax bracket next year, accelerating deductible expenses into the current year reduces this year's taxable income. Consider:

- Prepaying rent, insurance premiums, or subscriptions that cover January
- Purchasing needed equipment, supplies, or software before year-end
- Making charitable contributions in December rather than January
- Paying outstanding vendor invoices before December 31

## Strategy #2: Defer Income

If you are on the cash basis of accounting, you can defer income by delaying invoicing or holding checks until January. This pushes taxable income into the next year, potentially at a lower rate if you expect your income to decrease.

## Strategy #3: Maximize Retirement Contributions

Contributions to a SEP-IRA, SIMPLE IRA, or Solo 401(k) are deductible and reduce your taxable income dollar for dollar. For 2025, the contribution limits are generous:

| Retirement Account | 2025 Contribution Limit |
|-------------------|------------------------|
| SEP-IRA | Up to 25% of compensation, max $70,000 |
| Solo 401(k) | Up to $70,000 ($77,500 if age 50+) |
| SIMPLE IRA | $16,500 ($20,000 if age 50+) |

## Strategy #4: Take Advantage of 100% Bonus Depreciation

Under the OBBBA, 100% bonus depreciation has been restored. If you need equipment, vehicles, or technology for your business, purchasing before December 31 allows you to deduct the full cost in the current year rather than depreciating it over multiple years.

## Strategy #5: Review Your Business Structure

Year-end is an ideal time to evaluate whether your current business structure (sole proprietorship, LLC, S-corp, C-corp) is still optimal for your tax situation. An S-corp election, for example, can significantly reduce self-employment taxes for profitable businesses.

## Strategy #6: Harvest Tax Losses

If you have investments with unrealized losses, consider selling them before year-end to offset capital gains. This strategy, known as tax-loss harvesting, can reduce your capital gains tax liability.

The key to effective year-end tax planning is not waiting until December. Start your review in October, work with your accountant to project your full-year income and tax liability, and implement strategies with enough time to make a real difference.
